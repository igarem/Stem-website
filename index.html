About the Program

What is the Graduate Diploma in Education (STEM)?

The Graduate Diploma in Education (STEM) is a one-year teacher training program designed for graduates with academic backgrounds in Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics. It equips aspiring educators with the pedagogical knowledge and classroom skills needed to effectively teach STEM subjects in secondary schools across Papua New Guinea. Introduced in 2024, the program aligns with the National Department of Education’s (NDOE) goal of providing quality education across the country.

The program integrates theory with practice through structured coursework, field-based teaching practicums, and training in curriculum development, classroom management, and ICT in education. Graduates are eligible for teacher registration with the Teaching Service Commission and are prepared to teach both in urban and remote school settings.

Mission Statement

To develop qualified, innovative, and culturally responsive STEM educators who are committed to transforming education and contributing to national development through quality teaching and learning in Papua New Guinea.

Vision

To be a leading teacher education program in the Pacific region, producing empowered STEM teachers who inspire the next generation of scientists, engineers, technologists, and problem-solvers across PNG and beyond.

Future Plans

  • Introduce specialization tracks in Physics, Biology, Chemistry, Mathematics, and ICT.
  • Expand online and blended learning delivery modes to reach more remote students.
  • Strengthen partnerships with secondary schools for more immersive practicum experiences.
  • Collaborate with national and international agencies to offer scholarships and professional development opportunities.
  • Establish a STEM teaching resource center within the University of Goroka.

Organizational Structure

The Graduate Diploma in Education (STEM) program is managed and delivered through a collaborative academic structure that ensures quality teaching, supervision, and mentorship at all levels:

  • Program Director – Provides academic leadership and oversees curriculum development and quality assurance.
  • Head of Department (Education) – Manages overall delivery of education courses and liaises with university leadership.
  • STEM Course Coordinators – Responsible for the coordination of specific STEM subjects (e.g., Science, Mathematics, ICT).
  • Lecturers – Deliver subject-specific content, lead tutorials, and supervise student research and assessments.
  • Tutors and Teaching Assistants – Support lecturers with practical sessions, tutorials, and provide academic support to students.
  • Practicum Supervisors – Mentor students during their teaching practice in schools and provide feedback on performance.

Requirements

Applicants must meet the following criteria:

  • Be a non-school leaver (i.e. not applying directly after completing secondary school)
  • Meet the conditions of entry for UOG
  • Be a citizen of PNG and residing in PNG at the time of application
  • Not have been convicted of a criminal offence
  • Not have been terminated from a previous Australia Awards scholarship
  • Completed an undergraduate degree program in the field of science or mathematics between 2019 and 2023 with a minimum GPA of 2.6
  • Demonstrate a commitment to working as a secondary school teacher in STEM in PNG, including in rural and remote areas

Candidates must have a bachelor's degree in a STEM field. Additional documents required include academic transcripts, ID, and references.
